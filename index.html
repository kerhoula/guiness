Guinness Storehouse at St. James's Gate

Accessibility Review

We did both the Guinness Storehouse tour and Stoutie experience. The Storehouse tour was self-guided through the building, centering around an atrium shaped like a Guinness glass, and it ended with a Guinness at the top of the building overlooking the city. The Stoutie was the Storehouse tour with an extra Guinness glass with your photo laminated on the top of the foam.

Accessibility Concerns

Each Floor in the Storehouse Experience:

  • Lower Ground Floor: This floor serves as the entry point where you can find tickets, the group entrance, and audio guides.

  • Ground Floor: You can explore the core ingredients of Guinness: water, barley, and hops, and visit the retail store.

  • Floor 1: This level features the roasting and brewing processes, the cooperage, and an exhibition space.

  • Floor 2: This floor is dedicated to the sensory experience of the tasting rooms and includes the Arrol Suite.

  • Floor 3: Visitors can enjoy a dedicated cinema room and explore various advertising exhibits.

  • Floor 4: This level is home to the Guinness Academy, the Connoisseur Bar, and the Stoutie experience.

  • Floor 5: You can find dining and drinks at the 1837 Bar & Brasserie and Arthur's Bar.

  • Floor 7: The top floor features the Gravity Bar, offering panoramic views while you enjoy a pint.
